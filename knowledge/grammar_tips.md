# English Grammar Tips

## Present Perfect vs Past Simple

Use Past Simple for finished actions at a known time: "I visited London in
2019." Use Present Perfect when the time is not stated or the action affects
the present: "I have visited London" (at some point; the experience matters
now). Ukrainian and Russian have no perfect tenses, so this is one of the
hardest distinctions: both sentences translate the same way (я відвідав /
я посетил), and the difference must be learned from context.

## Articles: a, an, the

Slavic languages have no articles, so learners often drop them. Rules of
thumb: "a/an" introduces something new or non-specific ("I saw a dog");
"the" refers to something both speaker and listener can identify ("the dog
from next door"). No article with most plural or uncountable nouns used
generally ("Dogs are loyal", "Water is essential").

## Word Order

English word order is fixed: Subject - Verb - Object ("Anna reads books").
Unlike Ukrainian or Russian, you cannot reorder words for emphasis without
changing grammar. For emphasis English uses stress, cleft sentences ("It was
Anna who read the book"), or the passive voice.

## Conditionals

- Zero: general truths — "If you heat water, it boils."
- First: real future — "If it rains, I will stay home."
- Second: unreal present — "If I had time, I would learn French."
- Third: unreal past — "If I had studied, I would have passed."

The most common mistake is putting "will" or "would" into the if-clause:
say "If it rains", never "If it will rain".
