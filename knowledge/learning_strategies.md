# Learning Strategies

## Spaced Repetition

Review material at increasing intervals: after 1 day, 3 days, a week, a
month. Each successful recall strengthens the memory and earns a longer
interval; a failed recall resets it. Flashcards are the classic vehicle for
spaced repetition — review a topic in KuantorFlow shortly after adding
cards, then return to it with growing gaps.

## Active Recall Beats Re-reading

Testing yourself (trying to produce the translation before looking) is far
more effective than re-reading word lists. This is exactly what the
KuantorFlow quiz does: you see the English word and must type the
translation yourself. Struggling for a few seconds before answering is not
a problem — it is the mechanism that builds the memory.

## Learn Words in Context

A word learned inside a sentence sticks much better than an isolated pair.
When adding a flashcard, prefer entries that include example sentences, and
read the examples aloud. Grouping cards into meaningful topics (work,
travel, IT) also builds context — that is what KuantorFlow topics are for.

## How Many Words per Day

Quality beats quantity: 5-10 new words a day, reviewed consistently, out-
performs 50 words crammed once. A vocabulary of about 3000 well-chosen
word families covers roughly 95% of everyday English conversation.
