# KuantorFlow FAQ

## What is KuantorFlow

KuantorFlow is a web application for learning English, built for Ukrainian
and Russian speakers. It turns words and notes into flashcards with
translations, lets you browse them by topic, and quizzes you on them. It
runs at kuantorflow.pythonanywhere.com.

## How to Add Flashcards

Type an English word into the "Word" box on the main page and press
"Look up & save". The app looks the word up and proposes one card per part
of speech (noun, verb, adjective...) in a review popup — you can edit the
explanation and the Ukrainian/Russian translations, discard cards you don't
need with the cross, and press "Add" on the ones you want to keep. You can
also upload an .mht notes file exported from OneNote; lines shaped like
"word — explanation" become cards automatically.

## How the Quiz Works

Open a topic and press "Take quiz". The quiz shows each English word and
you type the translation. Use the language switch to practice Russian or
Ukrainian translations. Any of the stored comma-separated translation
variants counts as correct; case does not matter, and ё/е are treated as
equal. Wrong answers show the expected translation so you can learn from
mistakes.

## What Are Topics

Every card belongs to a topic — a named group like "IT", "travel" or
"general". Topics appear as clickable chips on the main page with the card
count. Grouping words by topic gives them context, which makes them easier
to remember, and lets you quiz yourself on one subject at a time.

## Access Keyword

The site is protected by a keyword screen. Enter the keyword you received
from the site owner to get access; the session then stays open in your
browser. This keeps the site and its database private.
